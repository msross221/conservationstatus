Evaluating the Conservation Status of Animal Species

This project evaluates the number of critically endangered (CR), endangered (EN), and vulnerable (VU) species globally. It explores global extinction patterns and focuses on endangerment patterns for animal taxonomic classes.

Data

The data for this project was accesss from the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) website . This data is publically available and was provided in a .csv and .pdf format. Because of the size of the data, we created a few basic visualizations for the entire dataset. We chose to focus the majority of the visualization and analysis on CR, EN, and VU species in animal classes. These taxonomic classes are easily recognizable (ex: mammals, reptiles, etc.) and recieve a large portion of conservation efforts because they are often charismatic. The figures and analyses shown in this project were created using Tableau.



Analysis

The first visualizations provide context for the current state of conservation by showing how many species are extinct and extinct in the wild for each animal taxonomic class.


The first figure shows the percentage fully extinct versus extinct in the wild out of species that are known to be extinct. Gastropods, birds, mammals, ray-finned fishes, and insects are among the animal classes that have the most extinct species both in the wild and in the wild and capativity. The data presented in these figures emphasizes the species loss experienced globally. While it does not directly relate to the research question, it evaluates overall endangerment of animal classes and provides context for further analysis of CR, EN, and VU species within each class.







Analysis


The first visualization directly compares the number of CR, EN, and VU animal species. It reveals that most animal species are VU, then EN, and lastly CR. Critically dangered species are most at risk while vulernable species are at the least amount of risk, so it is expected that there would be more VU species than CR species.



The beeswarm plot shows the proportion of each animal class that have a certain status out of all potential statuses (CR, EN, VU). The size of the dot is associated with the number of species in the class that have the certain status. This plot reveals that amphibians, gastropods, insects, and ray-finned fish have the largest total number of threatened species. This plot is the highlight of this project as it directly compares the number of CR, EN, and VU species in each class, and in this, evaluates endangerment patterns for the animal classes of interest.



Lastly, the stacked bar chart presents the same information from the beeswarm plot, but is a more easily digested manner. It essentially displays all of our data, including the number of species listed for each class and the percent composition of critically endangered, endangered, and vulnerable species in each class. It emphasizes the large differences in number of endangered species in animal classes. Ultimately, this data could be used to identify classes of conservation priority. It can be used to determine if it is beneficial to focus conservation efforts in classes that have the most CR, EN, and VU species or the concentrate resources on classes that can be saved and are not "too far gone".
